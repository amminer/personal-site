Hi,
I'm Amelia.

I'm a graduate student at Portland State University's Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science with previous experience in general biology. I'm interested in using computers to bridge the gaps in-between people as well as between us and the rest of the natural world.

I believe software is a uniquely powerful and flexible tool, opening doors to experiences people would otherwise be unable to have. Responsible and effective software engineering helps everyone to better understand one another and should facilitate easier, more comfortable living for everyone.

Some of my previous work includes...

Engineering / GIS / CAD

Working with environmental and stormwater engineering firms has given me insight into the ways that software is used in the field and incorporated into existing toolchains. I got my first taste of programming while trying to decipher Python scripts at my first stormwater engineering technician job many years ago, while I was in school for biology.

Clinical Testing Lab

With the importance of HIIPA compliance always in focus, clinical laboratory work gave me insight into how software can be built around privacy concerns such that it not only complies to standards but actually makes them easier to meet. Good informatics workstation programs and automation tools take cognitive load off employees and ultimately enhance the experiences of the lab's customers, employees, and management all at once.

Plant Tissue Culture

In my role as a tissue culture technician I had the opportunity to lead the digitization of an existing pen-and-paper system, which was valuable experience in so many ways. Working directly with the company's owner and the other technicians to set up a system that made sense for everyone as individuals and as a team was both challenging and satisfying.
