Hi,
I'm Amelia.

I'm a post-baccalaureate student at Portland State University's Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science. my existing Bachelor's degree is a B.S. Biology. My professional experience is highly varied, but mostly within a smattering of biological fields. I'm in the process of changing that - I'll make the leap to graduate student for the Winter 2023 semester, when I officially begin the Software Engineering track of PSU's M.S. Computer Science program.

I'm interested in using computers to bridge the gaps between people as well as between us and the rest of the natural world. I believe software is a uniquely powerful and flexible tool, opening doors to experiences people would otherwise be unable to have. Responsible and effective software engineering helps everyone to better understand one another and facilitates easier, more comfortable living for everyone.

Some of my previous work includes...

Engineering / GIS / CAD

Working with environmental and stormwater engineering firms has given me insight into the ways that software is used in the field and incorporated into existing toolchains. I got my first taste of programming while trying to decipher Python scripts at my first stormwater engineering technician job many years ago, while I was in school for biology.

Clinical Testing

With HIPAA always in focus, clinical laboratory work showed me how software can be built around privacy concerns (or any other specific need) such that it not only complies to standards but actually makes them easier to meet. Good informatics workstation programs and automation tools take cognitive load off employees and ultimately enhance the experiences of the lab's customers, employees, and management all at once.

Plant Tissue Culture

In my role as a tissue culture technician I had the opportunity to lead the digitization of an existing pen-and-paper stock management system, which was valuable experience in so many ways. Working directly with the company's owner and the other technicians to set up a system that made sense for everyone as individuals and as a team was both challenging and satisfying.

Some projects I've taken on...

Lightlapse

A Raspberry Pi based Python program to aid in managing environmental conditions for photosynthetic organisms.

I wrote this while beginning to teach myself Python/programming, GNU/Linux, and electronics. Eventually, I want to extend it to water & feed using gravity and solenoids, manage multiple light/watering zones, etc.

Asciifier

A Python script that creates text representations of images in the terminal using ansi escapes.

It adapts its output to the fit the size of the terminal window it runs in. Eventually I want to modify this so that it determines what character to use based on the brightness (or some similar metric) of the sector that character represents.

Animal Racing Simulator

A C++ game I wrote for CS202 but with a twist - watch out for predator-prey interactions!

I hope to add a mode where players earn winnings to buy and breed new animals with commensurate stats... Animal Racing Tycoon, anyone?
